Alpha Phi Omega

Zeta Chapter at Stanford University

be a leader, be a friend, be of service.

Who are we?

Alpha Phi Omega is a national co-ed service fraternity founded on the principles of Leadership, Friendship, and Service. With more than 400,000 members on over 375 campuses, Alpha Phi Omega continues to provide more service on more campuses than any other collegiate service organization.

What do we do?

Leadership

APO develops leaders through shared leadership. Members have the opportunity to serve as a chapter officer, or to plan service or fellowship events throughout the year. Leadership workshops are also offered throughout the year that not only develop skills that can be utilized within APO, but also in the professional world.

Friendship

Not only are APO members friends with one other, but also with the campus and community. We have multiple fellowship events that include boba runs, Special D's, and other off-campus retreats. Regional and Sectional conferences allow us to meet members of other chapters.

Service

Service is the basis of APO. The service program allows you to build leadership skills while making an impact on the Stanford community and beyond. We volunteer at on-campus events, such as Challah for Hunger, and organize annual events, such as Charity Auction.
